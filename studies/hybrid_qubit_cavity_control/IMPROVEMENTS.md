- **Waveform export still missing for `ConditionalPhaseSQR` and native effective interactions**: the follow-up now exposes pulse-backed waveforms for `SNAP` and `SQR`, but `ConditionalPhaseSQR`, `ConditionalDisplacement`, `JaynesCummingsExchange`, and `BlueSidebandExchange` still stop at the ideal/effective layer. This is now the main fairness limitation in the study. Next step: add waveform-bridge support and replay the best CP/native candidates through the compiled pulse path.
- **Structured optimization is still coherent and `n_tr=2`**: the new best structured candidate `L1d` reaches `F=0.9953` with `L=0.0065`, but this was found without direct `|f>` leakage or noisy objectives in the optimization loop. A noisy multilevel replay may reorder the top structured candidates.
- **Encoding scope is narrow**: all conclusions still refer to the strict Fock `|0>, |1>` encoding. The constructive shortcut and the native exchange family could look materially different in cat, binomial, or larger Fock encodings.

## What Was Tried and Did Not Work
- **Old depth-11 plateau interpretation**: the earlier conclusion that `D+R_q+SQR` was fundamentally limited near `F=0.92` was wrong. A single extra displacement gate increased fidelity to `0.9953`, so future agents should not assume the earlier leakage ceiling was structural.
- **Constructive `selective pi + broadband pi` shortcut**: best closed/noisy logical fidelity only `0.6020 / 0.5991`. The broadband pulse destroys logical selectivity too strongly in the present Fock window.
- **`ConditionalPhaseSQR` depth-plus-one rescue**: adding an extra displacement helps (`0.8684 -> 0.9135`) but does not close the gap to the SQR family.
- **GRAPE as a stable reference**: repeated seeds gave `0.9557`, `0.9141`, and `0.8960` strict fidelity for the same nominal setup. Treat GRAPE here as a seed-sensitive lower bound, not a stable production answer.
