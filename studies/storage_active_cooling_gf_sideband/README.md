# Sequential Active Cooling via the `|g,0_r,n_s> <-> |f,0_r,n_s-1> <-> |g,1_r,n_s-1>` Ladder

## Problem Class
DES, ANA, OPT

## Motivation
This study asks whether the local `cqed_sim` device can realize an experimentally useful storage-cooling primitive based on the ladder
\[
|g,0_r,n_s\rangle
\rightarrow
|f,0_r,n_s-1\rangle
\rightarrow
|g,1_r,n_s-1\rangle
\rightarrow
|g,0_r,n_s-1\rangle,
\]
for `n_s = 1,2,3,4`.

The required literature guide is **Fast Sideband Control of a Weakly Coupled Multimode Bosonic Memory** (`arXiv:2503.10623v1`). We use that paper to motivate:
- charge-driven sideband activation,
- photon-number-resolved spectroscopy and pulse calibration,
- bosonic enhancement of sideband rates,
- strong-drive Stark and Floquet considerations,
- and the use of smooth bump-style ramps instead of assuming a square pulse is optimal.

The study does **not** assume the paper's protocol can be copied unchanged. Instead, it adapts the paper's sideband-control logic to a different target ladder that uses the transmon `|f>` manifold plus a separate readout-dump step.

## Goals
1. Determine whether the Step A transition `|g,0_r,n_s> <-> |f,0_r,n_s-1>` is viable in the local device for `n_s <= 4`.
2. Determine whether the Step B transition `|f,0_r,n_s-1> <-> |g,1_r,n_s-1>` is selective and fast enough to support a cooling primitive.
3. Extract the dressed frequencies, state overlaps, and nearest competing lines that would guide experiment.
4. Compare multiple waveform families for both coherent steps, including square, Gaussian, DRAG-like, bump, phase-modulated bump, and composite-pulse-inspired controls.
5. Test whether strong-drive Stark shifts or Floquet dressing change the recommended calibration strategy.
6. Simulate full dissipative cooling cycles and repeated application on basis states and non-Fock inputs.
7. Produce experiment-facing recommendations together with a clear statement of what remains uncertain.

- `cqed_sim.core.frequencies.sideband_transition_frequency` and `basis_energy` for exact model-consistent spectroscopy.

## Analytic Preliminary
### Basis, Ordering, and Units
The report and all exported analysis use the user-requested state convention
\[
|q, n_r, n_s\rangle,
\]
where `q` is the transmon level, `n_r` is the readout occupation, and `n_s` is the storage occupation.

Important implementation note: the internal tensor ordering inside `cqed_sim` is `(transmon, storage, readout)`, but all human-facing labels are reordered before export so the report consistently uses `|q,n_r,n_s>`.

Internal `cqed_sim` frequencies are angular frequencies in `rad/s` and times are in `s`. The report quotes frequencies in `Hz`, `MHz`, or `GHz` after converting from internal units. The default rotating frame subtracts the bare transmon, storage, and readout frequencies.

### Drift Hamiltonian and Sign Conventions
The static model used in this study is
\[
\frac{H_0}{\hbar} =
\omega_q \hat n_q
+ \frac{\alpha}{2}\hat n_q(\hat n_q-1)
+ \omega_s \hat n_s
+ \omega_r \hat n_r
+ \chi_s \hat n_q \hat n_s
+ \chi_r \hat n_q \hat n_r
+ \chi_{sr} \hat n_s \hat n_r
+ \frac{K_s}{2}\hat n_s(\hat n_s-1)
+ \frac{K_r}{2}\hat n_r(\hat n_r-1).
\]

For the local device, `alpha < 0`, `chi_s < 0`, and `chi_r < 0`. In the present configuration, `chi_sr = K_s = K_r = 0`.

### Step A: Storage-to-Transmon Conversion
The target Step A resonance is
\[
|g,0_r,n_s\rangle \leftrightarrow |f,0_r,n_s-1\rangle.
\]
Within the dispersive drift model, this transition is not generated by a native dipole term; it must be activated by an effective storage red-sideband interaction. The corresponding resonance condition is
\[
\omega_A^{(n_s)}
= 2\omega_q + \alpha - \omega_s + 2\chi_s(n_s-1) - K_s(n_s-1).
\]

Projecting the effective storage sideband into the two-state manifold gives a matrix element proportional to `sqrt(n_s)`, so bosonic enhancement is expected. The extracted matrix elements are exactly `1`, `sqrt(2)`, `sqrt(3)`, and `2` for `n_s = 1..4`.

### Step B: Transmon-to-Readout Dump
The target Step B resonance is
\[
|f,0_r,n_s-1\rangle \leftrightarrow |g,1_r,n_s-1\rangle,
\]
with resonance condition
\[
\omega_B^{(n_s)}
= 2\omega_q + \alpha - \omega_r + 2\chi_s(n_s-1) - \chi_{sr}(n_s-1).
\]

In the present effective model the dump step is not naturally hybridized strongly enough to serve as passive transfer; it also requires explicit sideband activation. Because the readout starts in `|0_r>`, the dump matrix element does not acquire a `sqrt(n_s)` enhancement beyond the storage-dependent dispersive shift of the resonance.

### Connection to the Sideband Paper
The paper `arXiv:2503.10623v1` and this study share the same broad control logic:
- use charge-driven sideband physics to activate otherwise weak exchange processes,
- calibrate each photon-number-resolved line separately,
- exploit bosonic enhancement where the ladder structure permits it,
- and screen smooth ramps against naive square drives in the strong-drive regime.

The important differences are:
- the paper studies fast transmon-cavity SWAP-style control in a multimode memory, while this study targets a **cooling** primitive with an explicit dissipative readout sink,
- the present Step A transition uses the transmon `|f>` manifold and removes a storage photon,
- the present Step B is a separate transfer into the readout mode rather than the same cavity-mode exchange studied in the paper,
- and the local `cqed_sim` model exposes the relevant sidebands as **effective control operators**, not as a fully microscopic pump Hamiltonian.

Paper-motivated strong-drive checks were included explicitly. The storage scan found that a smooth `bump` envelope is the best Step A family for `n_s = 1, 3, 4`, while a `square` pulse wins only for `n_s = 2`. A calibration scan over `+-2 MHz` detuning found that the optimal detuning is `0.0 MHz` for every recommended Step A and Step B pulse in the effective model, so no resolved Stark shift appears inside this abstraction. A Floquet check shows that the dominant quasienergy doublet follows the expected bosonic splitting very closely, but every Floquet solve warns that truncation-boundary weight is noticeable, so those results are used qualitatively rather than as a final leakage prediction.

## Assumptions
- Device parameters are loaded from the editable local `cqed_sim` example `cQED_simulation/examples/sequential_sideband_reset.py` when available, with a hardcoded fallback only if the import fails.
- The working truncation `n_tr = 4`, `n_storage = 7`, `n_readout = 3` is sufficient for the reported results; nearby truncation changes are part of the validation pass.
- Readout decay is the dominant irreversibility channel for the cooling primitive.
- The effective sideband channels capture the relevant control-layer physics even though they do not expose a microscopic pump model.
- Floquet results are interpreted as evidence for target-doublet dressing and approximate strong-drive splitting, not as a precise final prediction of all off-manifold leakage.

## Compute & Resource Strategy
The study was organized around one medium-size end-to-end run plus a targeted convergence pass. The heaviest numerical tasks are the Step A and Step B amplitude-duration scans, followed by the open-system repeated-cycle simulations and figure generation.

Acceleration choices:
- keep the Hilbert space at the smallest converged truncation,
- reuse compiled sideband pulses and prepared objects where possible,
- export machine-readable artifacts so the report and notebook load saved results by default,
- and reserve Floquet analysis for a small set of recommended pulses rather than every scanned point.

Realized runtimes:
- full study rerun (`scripts/run_study.py`): `157.23 s`
- targeted convergence pass (`scripts/convergence_checks.py`): `13.80 s`
- reproducibility notebook execution (`python -m nbconvert --to notebook --execute --inplace reproducibility_notebook.ipynb`): `10.44 s`

## Expected Outcomes
Success for this completed study means answering the following questions with quantitative evidence:
- whether the ladder is viable through `n_s = 4`,
- what the dressed Step A and Step B frequencies are,
- which pulse families are best for each step,
- whether strong-drive effects materially change those recommendations,
- and whether repeated application cools the storage mode.

Headline outcomes achieved by the current artifact set:
- Step A frequencies: `6.804115034`, `6.798434192`, `6.792753350`, `6.787072508 GHz`
- Step B frequencies: `3.448825278`, `3.443144436`, `3.437463594`, `3.431782752 GHz`
- best Step A families: `bump` for `n_s = 1,3,4`, `square` for `n_s = 2`
- best Step B family: `cosine_squared` for all `n_s = 1..4`
- single-cycle success: `97.35%`, `97.29%`, `96.75%`, `96.20%`
- repeated-cycle final mean storage occupation:
  - basis ladder from `|g,0_r,4>`: `0.07574`
  - coherent-state test: `0.03129`
  - thermal-like test: `0.01251`

## Follow-Up Spectroscopy Extension
An experiment-facing spectroscopy follow-up was added after the main study to answer how the relevant lines should be found on hardware and what should actually be measured.

Key outputs:
- `data/spectroscopy_followup_summary.csv`
- `data/spectroscopy_followup_step_a.csv`
- `data/spectroscopy_followup_step_b.csv`
- `artifacts/spectroscopy_followup.json`
- `artifacts/spectroscopy_measurement_followup.md`
- `figures/spectroscopy_followup_step_a.{png,pdf}`
- `figures/spectroscopy_followup_step_b.{png,pdf}`

Headline conclusions from that extension:
- The correct first-pass Step A observable is `P_f` after a weak long storage-sideband probe, not an `X/Y` projection in the `g-f` manifold.
- A direct transmon `g-f` carrier is **not** the cooling transition because it keeps storage photon number fixed, but it is very useful as an auxiliary calibration for qutrit readout and for preparing `|f,0_r,n_s-1>` when calibrating Step B independently.
- If direct `f` readout is weak, the best fallback is a dump-assisted witness: scan Step A, then apply the calibrated Step B pulse and detect the induced readout occupation or integrated ringdown.
- In the effective model, weak-drive Step A and Step B spectroscopy peaks occur at `0.0 MHz` detuning relative to the dressed frequencies already identified in the main study.
- Because neighboring `n_s`-resolved lines remain only `5.680842 MHz` apart, coarse searches should use weak `~400 ns` probes before switching to the short high-fidelity control pulses.

## Known Limitations
- The local model validates **effective-control feasibility**, not microscopic hardware pump generation.
- Absolute pump-power calibration is unavailable because the model amplitude is an effective sideband rate, not a measured line power.
- The nearest competing `n_s` lines are only `5.680842 MHz` away, so spectral crowding remains a serious experimental challenge.
- The Floquet strong-drive check reproduces the expected target-doublet splittings, but the solver warns about truncation-boundary weight for every scanned case; leakage estimates beyond the target doublet should therefore be treated cautiously.
- The fixed `4/kappa_r` wait leaves residual readout occupation of order `1.7e-2`, so a faster repeated-cycle experiment may require a longer wait or active readout reset.
